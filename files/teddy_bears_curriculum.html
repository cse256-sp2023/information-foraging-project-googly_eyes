Each day we provide predictable routines that establish structure and consistency. We begin our day with free choice play. Children can choose different areas of the room to play in such as: housekeeping, art, science, manipulatives, writing, blocks, sensory table, and books. Clean up time is an important part of our day that teaches responsibility and organization. Next is “Circle Time” where we count the days on the calendar, sing songs and read books. We enjoy a healthy snack and then play on the playground to end our morning.

Fire Drill Practice

Last week we practiced what to do in case of a fire. The children calmly lined up and walked to our designated spot under a tree. The children did a fantastic job and then we enjoyed our time outdoors on the playground. We are proud of our Teddy Bears! In October the Fire Department will come for a visit to teach us more about Fire Safety.

Painting at the Art Easel

Every day the art easel provides our young artists with a unique sensory experience. Developmental skills practiced during easel painting include: Social/emotional as children talk about their creations next to one another Physical - using both small & large muscles of the arm and hand/eye coordination Cognitive - Problem Solving - How do I get the paint to the paper? What happens when I mix colors? What happens when I use a thinner or thicker brush? Why does some paint drip?

Spanish with Miss Colleen

Each week Miss Colleen comes to visit our classroom for Spanish. We sing songs, play games, and learn beginning concepts such as counting, colors, shapes, etc. Gracias Señora Colleen!

Music with Miss Christina

Last week we had our first Music class! We began with a Name & Circle song that helps us learn each others' names. We kept the beat with music sticks and loved moving our bodies to the rhythm! One of our favorites is "Put the Baby in the Bed" - a finger play song. We ended with a special version of "Twinke Twinkle Little Star.

Fire Safety

This week we talked about Fire Safety and the job of Fire Fighters. Using finger paints we created flames of red, yellow and orange. Finger painting strengthens our finger and hand muscles. It provides an excellent tactile experience and boosts creativity as we mix colors together and swirl them around our papers. A fire station, fire trucks of all sizes, and fire fighter dress-ups provided props for exciting pretend play.

On Friday the Clayton Fire Department visited WUNS!

The Teddy Bears enjoyed meeting Sparky and the fire fighters! Charlie demonstrated how to "Stop, Drop, & Roll." They talked to us about calling 911 and creating a safe meeting space if there is a fire. Due to the rain, we were not able to view their fire truck. Remember: "Don't Hide, Go Outside!"

Fall Leaves...

As the weather cools, the Teddy Bears are noticing all sorts of changes. Leaves of changing colors have begun to gather on our playground. We created beautiful leaves for our BIG classroom tree using both tissue paper and paint.

Sensory Play

Each week the Teddy Bears have endless opportunities to explore sensory experiences with water, sand, paint, baking ingredients, play-dough, and so much more. These experiences literally enhance every part of children's development.

Pumpkins

Pumpkins are popping up everywhere! Our science table is home to a nice sized pumpkin. The Teddy Bear love to touch the bright orange skin of the pumpkin. Using their fingers, they trace the vertical lines of the pumpkin. They notice the scratchy, dark green stem on top. Much of our art has been pumpkin themed! The skills used in these projects include:
Fine Motor Control: Painting with different size brushes, squeezing glue, cutting with scissors, tearing paper
Language: Talking about different pumpkins, comparing and using descriptive words
Math: Comparing different sizes of pumpkins in height and weight
Science: Where do pumpkins come from? Lifecycle of a pumpkin

Nature with Miss Becky

Our Science theme this month was "Scary Animals." By learning more about wild animals, they won't be so scary to us. Bats, spiders, owls, bees and snakes are some of the animals considered scary by many people. All of these animals are impportant to the environment and many help us by eating mosquitos, insects and mice.

Making Muffins

Last week the Teddy Bears made delicious banana, chocolate chip muffins. Our college helper, Maisie was a huge help in being in charge of the oven and ingredients. The children truly enjoyed stirring, pouring, measuring and, of course, eating the final product. Yummy!!! Cooking or baking with children provides an excellent experience to build self-confidence and some basic skills such as:
  • Math skills: measuring, counting, timing, ordering the steps (first, second, third)
  • Building vocabulary & reading skills
  • Fine Motor Skills: pouring, cracking eggs, stirring, scooping, assembling ingredients
  • Exploring senses: smelling, tasting, seeing, listening, and touching
  • Encouraging independence & trying new foods

Colors

We talked all about colors! We made a color wheel of the primary colors: red, blue and yellow. We glued on different colored objects to their matching color. We mixed colored paints together to see what happens. Our sensory table is a favorite area to explore colored water with a variety of objects that sink and float.

Hibernation

Miss Becky talked to our class about animals that hibernate in the winter. Winter can be hard for animals to stay warm or find food to eat. Hibernators include groundhogs, bats, reptiles, amphibians, and some insects. We got to see an actual snake, frog, and toad. We even learned a little song: \tHibernation, Time for hibernation.
